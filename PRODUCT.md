# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, hiring managers, and technical leads evaluating Aminudin Teguh Wijayanto ("Aw Teguh") for IT Support, SOC Analyst, or DevOps roles. They typically arrive from a job application, LinkedIn, or a referral, and they scan fast — often on mobile, often with several other candidates open. The job to be done: in under a minute, decide that this person is credible, technically deep, and worth contacting. A secondary audience is peers and the wider IT community who land here to gauge expertise before connecting.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio that establishes Aw Teguh's professional credibility across three linked disciplines — IT Support, Security Operations (SOC), and DevOps. It presents who he is, the experience and certifications that back the claims, representative projects, and a clear path to contact him. Success looks like: a recruiter finishes the page convinced of his depth and reaches out (or downloads the CV) without needing a separate document. Content is primarily in Indonesian, serving an Indonesian-language professional audience.

## Brand Personality

Technical and precise. Engineer-credible, confident, and substance-first — the design should signal depth in security and infrastructure without resorting to gimmicks. Three words: **precise, credible, grounded.** The tone is calm authority: it shows competence by being well-built and exact, not by shouting. Emotionally, a visitor should feel trust and the sense that this person sweats the details — exactly what you want from someone guarding your systems and pipelines.

## Anti-references

- **Generic template look.** Must not read as an untouched create-next-app / cookie-cutter dev-portfolio. No default starter scaffolding, no "I ran a generator and stopped" feel.
- **Corporate-bland.** No soulless, personality-free enterprise stock look. Professional must not mean forgettable.
- General AI-slop tells to avoid by reflex: purple-to-blue gradients, gradient text, identical icon+heading+text card grids, tracked uppercase eyebrows above every section, and the big-number hero-metric template.

## Design Principles

1. **Demonstrate, don't assert.** Craftsmanship in the build itself is the strongest proof of an engineer who values precision — the site is a work sample, not just a description of one.
2. **Scannable credibility.** Recruiters skim. Hierarchy, real artifacts (certs, experience, projects), and clear contact paths must surface the proof points fast, top to bottom.
3. **Substance over spectacle.** Every motion and visual choice earns its place by clarifying or reinforcing credibility. When in doubt, restraint wins.
4. **Security-engineer exactness.** Alignment, spacing, contrast, and states are correct because being correct is the whole point of the persona.
5. **Coherent identity.** A committed, recognizable visual system (the navy/steel/ice/snow palette and its dark mode) carried consistently — distinct from a template, never bland.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target **WCAG 2.1 AA**. Body text ≥ 4.5:1 contrast against its background (verify the steel/ice tones against both light snow and dark navy backgrounds); large text ≥ 3:1. Full keyboard navigation with visible focus states. Every framer-motion animation needs a `@media (prefers-reduced-motion: reduce)` alternative (crossfade or instant) — entrance and scroll-reveal motion must not be the only way content appears. Respect both light and dark themes for contrast.
